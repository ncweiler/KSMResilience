Surging Seas

In the fall of 2012, the storm surge caused by Hurricane Sandy devastaed the U.S. east coast, causing particularly severe damage to urban areas in New York and New Jersey.

The estimated damages to these areas were over 20 billion dollars.

In Sandy's aftermath, analysis revealed that much of the damage could have been reduced by investing in compartively cheaper structural mitigation measures.

Developing strong infrastructure to combat storm surges is vital for creating resilient coastal communities.
