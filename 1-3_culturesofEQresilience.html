Cultures of Earthquake Resilience

In 1906, San Francisco was devastated by a major earthquake followed by a firestorm that lasted for days.

Most of the city was destroyed and estimates suggest that thousands lost their lives.

New technologies are enabling structures to be more resilient to future seismic shocks.
