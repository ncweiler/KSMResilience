In 1906, San Francisco was devastated by a major earthquake followed by a firestorm that lasted for days.

Most of the city was destroyed and estimates suggest that thousands lost their lives.

New technologies are enabling structures to be more resilient to future seismic shocks.

The following video highlights 2 ways the newly constructed San Francisco Bay Bridge
is designed to bend and shift, but not break in a major quake:

These technologies can mean the difference between life and death.

This video demonstrates the large-scale testing of earthquake resistent technologies
in a full-scale model hospital building at the University of California, San Diego:

Despite investments earthquake-proofing, an event like the 1906 quake would likely be a major catastrophe.
