A City Underwater

In the spring of 2008, tornados, heavy rains, and flooding ravaged eastern Iowa.

Many in Cedar Rapids, a city of 125,000 people along the banks of the Cedar River, remembered the record floods of 1993, when the river crested at 22.5 feet and caused extensive damage throughout the city.

The city had spent years preparing to prevent such 100-year flood events, but they did not expect to face one again so soon.

As the rains continued through May and June, 2008, local leaders began to monitor for potential flooding as the Cedar River began to rise.

On June 13 the river crested at over 31 feet.

The city's years of preparations could not withstand such a monsterous "500-year" flood.

The river flowed through 1300 city blocks, inundating the downtown business district, and displacing nearly 18,000 people.

Despite their losses, the people of Cedar Rapids came together to save what they could.

More than 1000 citizens responded to a public service announcement requesting help protecting the city's last uncontaminated water well.

By sandbagging the area around the well, the volunteers saved the last of the city's clean water supply.

Unfortunately, a similar all-night volunteer effort was not able to save Mercy Hospital from flooding.

But when the flood waters rose too high, volunteers coordinated with medical professionals to help move patients to nearby hospitals.
